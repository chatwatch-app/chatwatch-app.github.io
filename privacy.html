ChatWatch Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026 — Beta version

What ChatWatch does

ChatWatch connects to public live-stream chat (Kick.com, Twitch, and YouTube Live) and relays messages to your Wear OS watch over the Wearable Data Layer. All processing happens on your own devices. ChatWatch does not operate any servers or store your data in the cloud.

Data we collect

ChatWatch stores the following data locally on your phone only:

  • The channels you have connected to (slug, chatroom/video ID, platform).
  • The viewer username you provided (used to highlight your own callouts in chat).
  • Your app preferences (e.g. haptic settings, font size, crash-report opt-out, subscription state).

None of this data is transmitted to ChatWatch or any third party.

Crash reports

By default, ChatWatch sends anonymous crash reports to Sentry so we can find and fix bugs that affect real users. The data is limited to:

  • Stack traces when the app crashes.
  • Connection success / failure events (no channel names).
  • Device model, OS version, and app version.

No personally identifiable information (PII) is collected or sent. You can opt out at any time in the app's settings sheet ("Share crash reports" toggle). Once disabled, no further data is sent from either the phone or the paired watch.

Third-party services

  • Kick.com — ChatWatch reads Kick's public chat API. Your use of Kick is governed by Kick's Terms of Service.
  • Twitch — ChatWatch reads Twitch's public chat (IRC). Your use of Twitch is governed by Twitch's Terms of Service.
  • YouTube Live — ChatWatch reads YouTube's public live-chat data. Your use of YouTube is governed by YouTube's Terms of Service.
  • Sentry — anonymous crash and error reporting (on by default, user can opt out). See Sentry's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Play Billing — handles the subscription and free trial. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Play Services / Wearable Data Layer — used to communicate between your phone and watch.

Data deletion requests

On-device data — your channel history, settings, viewer username, and subscription state are stored only on your phone and paired watch. Uninstalling ChatWatch removes all of this data. We do not have copies of it on any server.

Anonymous crash reports — if you've left the crash-report opt-in enabled (Settings → Share crash reports), Sentry receives anonymous stack traces tagged with a random per-install identifier. No personal information is sent.

To request deletion of any anonymous crash data tied to your install, email byerikolsson@gmail.com. We'll delete any matching events from Sentry within 30 days. You can also stop future data from being sent at any time by disabling the "Share crash reports" toggle in the app's settings.

Children

ChatWatch is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.
